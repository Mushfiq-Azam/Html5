My trip to ISSB was amazing

Published on: June 25,2025

Introduction: My Trip to ISSB

Hello and welcome to my website — My Trip to ISSB! This is where I share my full experience of attending the Inter Services Selection Board (ISSB). From the moment I got the call letter to the final day, I’ve documented it all — raw and real. Whether you’re a future candidate or just curious, I hope my journey helps and inspires you.

How I Prepared

Preparation wasn’t just about studying — it was about discipline. I focused on four areas: Physical fitness (running, pushups, situps) Mental strength (daily meditation and confidence-building) Knowledge (current affairs, defense-related topics) Communication (group discussions and public speaking) I also practiced ISSB-specific tests like WAT, TAT, and Situation Reaction Tests regularly.

My Day-by-Day ISSB Experience

Day 1: Reporting & document checking — everything felt serious from the start. Day 2: Psychological tests — rapid-fire writing, time pressure, and full focus. Day 3: GTO tasks — teamwork, leadership, and staying calm under pressure. Day 4: Interview — personal questions, quick thinking, and honesty. Day 5: Conference — the final moment. A mix of fear, hope, and satisfaction.

What I Learned

The ISSB journey taught me a lot more than I expected: Discipline is not optional. Confidence is key — even more than having the right answer. Teamwork matters as much as individual performance. Most importantly: Know yourself, believe in yourself.

Tips for Future Candidates

Here’s my quick advice if you’re planning to go to ISSB: Be yourself. Don’t fake anything. Stay fit — physically and mentally. Improve your speaking skills. Stay updated on national and international issues. Respect your teammates. You’re not competing against them — you’re being tested with them.
